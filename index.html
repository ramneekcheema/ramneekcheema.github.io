About Me

I am a researcher and problem-solver at the intersection of climate, public health, and data. I have a Honours BSc in Biomedical Sciences with a specialization in Bioinformatics from the University of Waterloo. I have also completed an MSc in Biology with a specialization in Environmental & Chemical Toxicology at the University of Ottawa, where I built an optimal diet and economic model that integrates fish and methylmercury projections in the Pacific Northeast to inform food security for Haida and Tla’amin Nations. I use Python, R, and SAS to translate complex environmental, dietary, and socioeconomic data into clear, actionable insights and I love turning those insights into stakeholder-ready stories and workshops. Alongside my academic work, I have led implementation projects in industry, taught and mentored students, and helped convene multi-hundred-person research events. I care about climate resilience, equity, and evidence-based decisions that improve real lives.
